# Magnetism Formulas

## Magnetic Force on a Current-Carrying Wire

### Formula
$ F = BIL\sin	heta $

### Variables
- $ F $: Magnetic force (Newtons)
- $ B $: Magnetic field strength (Teslas)
- $ I $: Current (Amperes)
- $ L $: Length of the wire in the magnetic field (meters)
- $ 	heta $: Angle between the current direction and the magnetic field

### Usage
Calculates the force experienced by a current-carrying wire placed in a magnetic field.

## Magnetic Force on a Moving Charge

### Formula
$ F = qvB\sin	heta $

### Variables
- $ F $: Magnetic force (Newtons)
- $ q $: Magnitude of the charge (Coulombs)
- $ v $: Velocity of the charge (m/s)
- $ B $: Magnetic field strength (Teslas)
- $ 	heta $: Angle between the velocity vector and the magnetic field vector

### Usage
Calculates the force experienced by a moving charged particle in a magnetic field.

## Magnetic Field due to a Long Straight Wire

$ B = \frac{\mu_0 I}{2\pi r} $

## Faraday's Law of Induction

### Formula
$ \mathcal{E} = -N\frac{\Delta\Phi_B}{\Delta t} $

### Variables
- $ \mathcal{E} $: Induced electromotive force (EMF) (Volts)
- $ N $: Number of turns in the coil
- $ \Phi_B $: Magnetic flux (Weber)
- $ t $: Time (seconds)

### Usage
Describes how a changing magnetic field can induce an electromotive force (and thus an electric current) in an electrical conductor.

# Kinematics Formulas

## Displacement

### Formula
$ \Delta x = x_f - x_i $

### Variables
- $ \Delta x $: Displacement (change in position)
- $ x_f $: Final position
- $ x_i $: Initial position

### Usage
Used to calculate the change in position of an object.

## Average Velocity

### Formula
$ v_{avg} = \frac{\Delta x}{\Delta t} = \frac{x_f - x_i}{t_f - t_i} $

### Variables
- $ v_{avg} $: Average velocity
- $ \Delta x $: Displacement
- $ \Delta t $: Change in time
- $ x_f $: Final position
- $ x_i $: Initial position
- $ t_f $: Final time
- $ t_i $: Initial time

### Usage
Used to calculate the average rate of change of position over a time interval.

## Constant Acceleration - Final Velocity

### Formula
$ v_f = v_i + at $

### Variables
- $ v_f $: Final velocity
- $ v_i $: Initial velocity
- $ a $: Acceleration
- $ t $: Time

### Usage
Used to find the final velocity of an object undergoing constant acceleration.

## Constant Acceleration - Displacement

### Formula
$ \Delta x = v_i t + \frac{1}{2}at^2 $

### Variables
- $ \Delta x $: Displacement
- $ v_i $: Initial velocity
- $ a $: Acceleration
- $ t $: Time

### Usage
Used to find the displacement of an object undergoing constant acceleration.

## Constant Acceleration - Final Velocity Squared

### Formula
$ v_f^2 = v_i^2 + 2a\Delta x $

### Variables
- $ v_f $: Final velocity
- $ v_i $: Initial velocity
- $ a $: Acceleration
- $ \Delta x $: Displacement

### Usage
Used to find the final velocity when time is not known but displacement is.

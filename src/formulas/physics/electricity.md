# Electricity Formulas

## Ohm's Law

### Formula
$ V = IR $

### Variables
- $ V $: Voltage (Volts)
- $ I $: Current (Amperes)
- $ R $: Resistance (Ohms)

### Usage
Relates voltage, current, and resistance in an electrical circuit.

## Electric Power

### Formula
$ P = VI = I^2R = \frac{V^2}{R} $

### Variables
- $ P $: Electric Power (Watts)
- $ V $: Voltage (Volts)
- $ I $: Current (Amperes)
- $ R $: Resistance (Ohms)

### Usage
Calculates the rate at which electrical energy is converted to another form of energy (e.g., heat, light).

## Resistors in Series

### Formula
$ R_{eq} = R_1 + R_2 + ... + R_n $

### Variables
- $ R_{eq} $: Equivalent resistance
- $ R_1, R_2, ..., R_n $: Individual resistances

### Usage
Used to find the total resistance of resistors connected in series.

## Resistors in Parallel

### Formula
$ \frac{1}{R_{eq}} = \frac{1}{R_1} + \frac{1}{R_2} + ... + \frac{1}{R_n} $

### Variables
- $ R_{eq} $: Equivalent resistance
- $ R_1, R_2, ..., R_n $: Individual resistances

### Usage
Used to find the total resistance of resistors connected in parallel.

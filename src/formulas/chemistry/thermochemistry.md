# Thermochemistry Formulas

## Enthalpy Change

### Formula
$ \Delta H = H_{products} - H_{reactants} $

### Variables
- $ \Delta H $: Enthalpy change (Joules or kJ)
- $ H_{products} $: Enthalpy of products
- $ H_{reactants} $: Enthalpy of reactants

### Usage
Used to calculate the heat absorbed or released during a chemical reaction at constant pressure.

## Heat Transfer

### Formula
$ q = mc\Delta T $

### Variables
- $ q $: Heat energy (Joules)
- $ m $: Mass of substance (grams)
- $ c $: Specific heat capacity (J/g·°C)
- $ \Delta T $: Change in temperature (°C)

### Usage
Used to calculate the heat transferred when a substance changes temperature.

## Gibbs Free Energy

### Formula
$ \Delta G = \Delta H - T\Delta S $

### Variables
- $ \Delta G $: Gibbs free energy change (Joules or kJ)
- $ \Delta H $: Enthalpy change (Joules or kJ)
- $ T $: Temperature (Kelvin)
- $ \Delta S $: Entropy change (J/K)

### Usage
Used to predict the spontaneity of a chemical reaction. A negative $\Delta G$ indicates a spontaneous reaction.
